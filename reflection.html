Personal Reflection

I chose to major in Computer Science because it is one of the largest growing fields right now. It all started with Robotics and I also took many Computer Science classes in high school. And, I found out that I am pretty good at it. Another part that encouraged me is that my cousin has a degree in Computer Science and works for the National Security Agency in D.C.

When I got to the University of Missouri, I didn’t know how to code in C. But, it is very similar to Java and JavaScript which I know pretty well. Overall, it has strengthened my understanding in programming. The coursework at mizzou has challenged me in many ways. It has forced me to study for exams and manage my time effectively. Beyond that, I joined a fraternity that has helped me adjust to college life while still having a strong focus on my academic goals.

My long-term career goal is to of course graduate and get a job in the cybersecurity field, where I can protect systems, networks, and data from threats. I want to also follow my cousin and work for the government for my first job and hopefully use that to get into the private sector. Before I graduate, I want to get an internship over a summer to gain more experience in a professional environment. I also want to maintain a GPA of 3.5 or higher.

Looking ahead, I want to keep on learning as I get older and be able to adapt to technology as it will always change and improve. My goal is to not only be a skilled programmer but be a professional that will work hard. Ultimately, I hope to build a career that allows me to grow as a person that will eventually settle in Denver, Colorado.
